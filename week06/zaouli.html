Zaouli is a traditional dance and cultural practice of the Guro people (also spelled Gouro) from the central-western region of Côte d'Ivoire (Ivory Coast).

HISTORY & ORIGINS

Origin of Zaouli

Zaouli dance originates from the Guro people of central Côte d’Ivoire (Ivory Coast) in West Africa.
It was created in the 1950s, making it a relatively recent tradition compared to other African mask dances.According to oral tradition, the dance was inspired by a young girl named Djela Lou Zaouli, whose beauty and presence captivated her community.
The name "Zaouli" means "daughter of Djela" in the Guro language. Although the exact details vary from village to village, the Zaouli mask was created to honor this girl and quickly became a symbol of cultural pride and artistic expression among the Guro.

History of Zaouli

Since its creation, the Zaouli dance has played an important role in Guro society. It is performed during funerals, celebrations, community festivals, and important social gatherings.
